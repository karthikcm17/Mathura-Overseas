Counselling
Counselling Timeline Shifted Too

MCC counselling registration is expected to open around July 21, 2026 (tentative). The whole cycle is running roughly two months behind a typical year — check mcc.nic.in for the confirmed schedule.

The delay traces back to the original NEET UG 2026 exam being cancelled and re-conducted, which pushed the entire results-to-counselling chain later than usual. In a typical year, AIQ Round 1 registration opens within days of results; this year the gap is closer to a month. Expect Round 1 allotment in late July, Rounds 2/3 through August–September, and the Stray Vacancy round around October — all still provisional until MCC's official notification. If you're also considering an abroad option as a backup, most of our partner universities' intakes run later in the year anyway, so this delay doesn't meaningfully shrink your options there.

Tips & Guidance
Marks vs Rank, Explained

In 2025, 502 marks was worth roughly AIR 50,000. This year, it takes 535 marks to reach that same rank — don't compare your score to last year's "safe" numbers.

This isn't normal year-to-year variance — it's a structural shift because the Re-NEET was a genuinely different exam (different paper, different difficulty curve, different candidate pool of 22+ lakh) than the original cancelled attempt or last year's NEET. The practical takeaway: if you're using last year's cutoff lists or a friend's "I got X marks and got into Y college last year" story to gauge your chances, you're working with the wrong reference point. Wait for MCC's actual round-wise closing ranks once counselling starts, and treat any pre-counselling prediction — including tools that claim to predict your college — with real caution this year specifically.

NEET News
Why This Year Is Different

The original NEET UG 2026 exam (May 3) was cancelled after paper leak allegations. NTA conducted a fresh Re-NEET on June 21, 2026, with 22+ lakh candidates appearing.

This is the first time in recent years NTA has had to fully re-conduct NEET UG at this scale, and it has knock-on effects beyond just the exam date: a compressed timeline between result and counselling, a counselling schedule pushed roughly two months later than usual, and a genuinely different score distribution (see our "Marks vs Rank" update) that makes historical comparisons unreliable this cycle. If you were counting on a particular score or rank being "safe" based on past years, it's worth re-checking that assumption against this year's actual numbers once MCC publishes round-wise data.

Tamil Nadu MDS Admission 2026-27: Prospectus Released

Applications for MDS admissions open 13 July, 12:00 Noon, close 22 July, 11:00 AM. Based on NEET MDS-2026 rank. Apply via tnmedicalselection.net.

The Selection Committee, Directorate of Medical Education & Research, Tamil Nadu, has released the prospectus covering admission to Government Dental Colleges, Government seats in Self-Financing colleges, and Management & NRI quota seats. Eligibility requires the qualifying percentile in NEET MDS-2026 (50th percentile for General category, 40th for reserved categories). All applications must be submitted online only, with all required documents uploaded alongside the form — incomplete uploads are a common rejection reason, so read the prospectus carefully before starting. Counselling runs across four rounds (Round 1, Round 2, Mop-up, and Stray Round), with exact dates to be notified separately. A separate prospectus applies specifically to Management & NRI quota seats in Self-Financing Dental Colleges, with its own eligibility and document requirements for NRI candidates.

Official Advisory
Philippine MD Degrees Confirmed NMC-Compliant

The Embassy of India in Manila confirmed the 54-month MD programme at CHED-recognised Philippine institutions meets India's Foreign Medical Graduate Licentiate requirements. Read the advisory.

Per CHED's May 2025 letter, compliance requires: the mandatory subjects specified in NMC's November 2021 notification, fully English-medium instruction, a 12-month internship at the same institution/hospital, and a Certificate of Registrability. The Philippine House of Representatives separately passed Resolution No. 73 in March 2026 endorsing the same compliance. One nuance worth knowing: practicing within the Philippines itself still requires passing the local Physician Licensure Examination, and only if India extends equivalent reciprocity to Filipino graduates — that reciprocity guidance is still awaited. This does not affect graduates planning to practice in India, the US, UK, Canada, Australia or several other countries, which follow their own separate licensing processes. Always verify NMC compliance for your specific chosen university before enrolling, since this advisory covers CHED-recognised institutions generally, not every individual college automatically.
